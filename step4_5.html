Step 4: Create Your Scenarios

Olivia Carter

Scenario: The Last-Minute Sublet

With finals coming up, Olivia also needs to sublet her apartment for summer break. She logs into Craigslist and starts crafting a listing for her studio near campus. She struggles with the plain-text formatting, unsure if line breaks or links will render properly. She previews the post and realizes it’s missing images—she didn’t know uploading them wasn’t automatic. Then, while browsing other listings to compare, she notices hers is already buried under newer posts. She’s frustrated by the lack of visibility and limited filtering tools. Olivia decides to manually repost every few days and include her email instead of using Craigslist’s reply system, which she finds unreliable.

David Lin

Scenario: Need Help, Yesterday

It’s Monday morning, and David urgently needs to hire a temporary worker after one of his employees called in sick for the week. He logs onto Craigslist to post a job ad. The form asks for more details than he expected—location, compensation type, and email verification. He quickly fills it out, but accidentally selects the wrong category. His post doesn’t show up where he expected. David has to go back and edit, but finds the interface clunky and slow. When responses come in, half are spam. He’s frustrated, thinking, “Why isn’t there a filter for serious applicants?”

Step 5: Write Your Test Plan

1. Test Goals

This test uses Whitney Quesenbery’s 5Es to evaluate the usability of Craigslist.org:

  • Effective: Can users accomplish their goals?
  • Efficient: Can they do it quickly?
  • Engaging: Is the experience enjoyable?
  • Error-tolerant: Can they recover from mistakes?
  • Easy to learn: Is the system intuitive for new users?

2. Scope of the Project

  • Number of users: 3 participants
  • Session length: 45 minutes each
  • Environment: Remote or in-person (desktop preferred)
  • Device: Laptop or desktop browser

3. Tasks / Scenarios

  • Task 1 (Olivia Carter): Post a listing for a summer sublet apartment with photos and contact info.
  • Task 2 (Olivia Carter): Search and compare two part-time job listings in media/marketing in your location.
  • Task 3 (David Lin): Post a job ad for a retail clerk and verify it appears correctly.
  • Task 4 (David Lin): Search for and compare two shelving units or desks within a 20-mile radius.
  • Task 5 (Neutral User): Find a free item (e.g., couch or TV), contact the poster, and explain why.
